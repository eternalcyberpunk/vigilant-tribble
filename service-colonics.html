What it is

Warm water. Gently flushed. Full colon.

Colon hydrotherapy — also called colon lavage or a colonic — is a safe, drug-free method of removing waste from the large intestine through repeated, gentle flushing with warm filtered water.

During a session, you lie comfortably on a table. Warm water flows in through a small, disposable speculum and returns out through a separate waste tube that carries away impacted feces, mucus, and toxins. You'll see the waste pass through a special viewing window — the entire system is closed and completely odorless.

We use state-of-the-art FDA-approved equipment with a triple-filtration system — carbon and UV filters, plus fully disposable tubing. Every session is monitored from start to finish by a therapist certified by the International Association of Colon Hydrotherapists (I-ACT), using a slow-fill technique for maximum comfort.

Honest answers

Questions we hear every week.

Is colon hydrotherapy safe?
Yes. We use FDA-approved equipment with disposable, single-use components and sterile procedures — there's no danger of contamination. In fact, a professional colonic is significantly safer than a home enema. All sessions are performed by I-ACT certified therapists who monitor water pressure (never more than 2 psi) and temperature throughout.
Does it hurt?
No. Clients assist with the initial insertion to ensure comfort, and the filling pressure is very low (minimum 1/4 psi, max 2 psi). The water temperature and flow are constantly adjusted with your feedback. Most clients describe the experience as mildly pressurizing, not painful.
How does it compare to an enema?
An enema only reaches the lower part of the colon. Colon hydrotherapy bathes the full length of the colon — approximately five to five and a half feet — over a longer period, which also helps rehydrate the colon and soften any hardened waste adhering to the walls.
How many sessions will I need?
It varies by person and depends on your overall health and lifestyle. For first-time clients we recommend an initial series of six to ten visits over several weeks. After that, most clients return for a seasonal cleanse — four times a year, with the change of each season, works well for many people.
Is it habit-forming?
No. The point of cleansing is to allow the colon to rest and rejuvenate so it can restore its natural peristalsis. The colon can't heal when it's constantly working to eliminate accumulated waste, gas, and toxins.
Can constipation occur even if I have regular bowel movements?
Yes. Mucoid plaque and trapped gas can interfere with natural peristaltic action, resulting in incomplete though frequent bowel movements. This buildup can cause problems throughout the digestive tract and the rest of the body.
Should I see my doctor first?
It's always a good idea to consult your doctor, especially if you have any medical conditions, but a medical examination isn't required for most people. Certain medical conditions do make colon hydrotherapy inadvisable — we'll ask about your health history at your first visit.
Does my menstrual period affect the treatment?
No. If anything, a colonic during your period can be helpful — your body is already in a cleansing mode, and flushing the colon can reduce abdominal pressure and cramping. Your menstrual flow won't interfere with the treatment.
Can I work after a session?
Yes, in most cases. Many clients report feeling refreshed and energized. If you're actively detoxifying from other things, you may feel tired for a few hours as your body releases toxins — if that's a concern for a particular day, we recommend scheduling in the evening or on a weekend.
Will this disturb my normal gut flora?
Briefly, yes — cleansing reduces both beneficial flora and electrolytes. That's why every session ends with a complimentary electrolyte drink and a probiotic supplement to help reintroduce healthy flora. You can also support recovery at home with kefir or Essential Duo.
Prep for your visit

A little preparation goes far.

None of this is strictly required, but each of these will meaningfully improve your experience and the effectiveness of your session.

  • The day before, eat fruit and vegetables if possible — this softens everything and makes the session easier.
  • Drink 8–10 glasses of water the day before and the day of your visit.
  • Avoid eating for two hours before your appointment — we do gentle abdominal massage during the session.
  • Skip carbonated beverages the day before — they add gas to the colon.
  • Arrive 15 minutes before your scheduled time, relaxed and unrushed.
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing — nothing restrictive around the waist.
