01

Before your first visit

Do I need a doctor's referral?
No — we don't require a referral. That said, if you have a significant medical condition, we always recommend checking with your doctor before your first visit. Some conditions make colonics inadvisable.
What should I wear?
Loose, comfortable clothing — nothing restrictive around the waist. You'll change into a gown for the session itself.
Can I eat before my appointment?
Avoid eating for about two hours before your appointment. We do gentle abdominal massage during the session, and a full stomach makes it less comfortable. Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
How long will my first visit take?
Plan for about an hour. Your first visit includes a brief consultation and case history, the session itself (30–40 minutes), and time for a short rest and a replenishing drink afterward.

Pricing & policies

Does insurance cover colonics?
No. Colon hydrotherapy is considered a wellness service, not a medical procedure, and isn't covered by health insurance. We accept Visa, Mastercard, Discover, American Express, and debit cards.
What's the cancellation policy?
We ask for at least 24 hours notice for any cancellation or reschedule. Cancellations within 24 hours are charged 50% of the service price; no-shows are charged the full amount. This lets us offer your spot to someone on the waitlist.
Do packages expire?
No — use them at whatever pace works for you. Packages are non-refundable and non-transferable, but they don't have expiration dates.
Can I buy a gift card?
Yes. Call us at (909) 603-9200 to purchase by phone. We can mail the card directly or email a printable certificate. Gift cards expire 90 days from purchase.
Is the $65 Wednesday rate available to everyone?
It's limited to new clients, for their first colonic visit. A credit card reservation is required to book. After your first Wednesday session, standard pricing applies — though package discounts are often a better deal for anyone planning to come back.
03

During & after

Will I be able to drive home?
Yes. Most clients feel lighter and energized after a session. A small percentage feel tired as their body releases toxins — if you're in an active detox phase, consider booking later in the day just in case.
What should I eat afterward?
Easy on the digestion for the rest of the day. Soups, broths, steamed vegetables, fruit, plenty of water. Avoid alcohol, heavy meat, and fried food. We send you home with a probiotic and electrolyte drink.
How will I feel the next day?
Most clients feel notably better — lighter, clearer, more energized. Some experience mild flu-like symptoms in the first day or two as toxins fully clear. This is normal, especially in early sessions, and resolves quickly.
When should I come back?
For first-time clients, we recommend a series of six to ten visits spread over several weeks. After that, most clients return seasonally — four times a year — as a maintenance cleanse. Your therapist will recommend a rhythm that fits your body and your life.

A few others

Do you offer consultations without a session?
Yes. If you'd like to meet us, see the space, and ask questions before committing to an appointment, please call and we'll find a time. No charge.
Is the clinic private?
Completely. Each session happens in a private treatment room with your therapist. The closed-system equipment is 100% odorless, and you'll never see or hear another client during your appointment.
Can my partner or friend come with me?
They're welcome to bring you to the clinic, but for privacy they'll need to wait in our reception area during the session itself.
Do you work with clients prepping for a colonoscopy?
Yes — many clients use colon hydrotherapy as part of their prep. Call us at least a week before your procedure so we can coordinate timing with your doctor's instructions.
